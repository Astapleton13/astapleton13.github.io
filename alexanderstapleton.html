Webdesign/UI/UX/Logo Design

The Setup

To begin every project, I start with a pen and paper. I believe that gives me the most freedom when coming up with multiple designs for a project. I want this website to be a clean design to draw attention to the work that I display here, not to the site itself.

Logos

As with the website itself, I designed the Logo with the same feel; clean, minimal, and straight to the point. Alexanderstapleton.com is a site about who I am and what displays more meaning about a person than their name? The designs went from using my full name to breaking it down into parts to finally coming to my initials, who I am in a condensed form, inside a structure reminiscent of the pallet in which I work.

Wireframes

One page? Multipage? Questions I mulled over for a long time. Originally I thought about having my major nav components on different pages, but having so many pages would be going against my original plan to keep a clean and minimal portfolio page for people to view my work. That's where the one-page design came to mind, black and white single scroll page that would have everything a potential client would need to see to get a feel for who I am, the work I do, and if they'd like to hire me.

Development

Bootstrap! Either you love it, or you hate it, at least that's what I've heard. Personally, I think it's great and can speed up the development process tremendously. That along with its clean grid system is why I chose to use it as the framework from which I created this site, to show potential employers my familiarity with it.
