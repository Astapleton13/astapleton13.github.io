My Process

Understanding

First things first I need to understand the problem at hand. What is currently being done? What are other organizations doing to solve a similar problem. No two companies will ever have the same problem but I can draw ideas from how other people have solved similar dilemas. After that I need to understand the users, who are they, what are they doing, what do they expect to happen in this instance, and what is actually happening that is creating a pain point for the user. All this research goes into user stories and personas for the user and moodboards for the company research that I come up with. If the client I am working with has a pattern library already setup then I use that to facilitate a lot my decisions later on in the process. User flows and journey maps go here as well

Ideation

Now that I have a frame of reference and a better understanding of the problem at hand it's now time to come up with ideas. This is usually done depending on the project by a series of quick sketches on paper or a whiteboard. The concept here is to get as many ideas down as possible and not focus on a single solution too early on in the process.

Prototyping/Wireframing

My next step when possible is to have the designs created in the Ideation process looked over by other people involved in the process, be it other designers, a PO, developers, or a stakeholder. I then take the top 2-3 designs and continue to refine those designs into wireframes or working prototypes

Review/Iteration

Once I have wireframes or prototypes setup the last step in my process is to get feedback from users. This can be done in many ways, recently I've been using Optimal Workshop to send out different types of surveys depending on what the prototype was and what kind of information I was looking to get out of the test. Once I have the results back from the survey I take that information and using the design the users responded most favorably to I iterate on that design incorporating the feedback gained and then go through the process again as needed until I have a design that is both beneficial to the business and usable by the customer/user
